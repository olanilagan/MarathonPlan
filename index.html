USA Holiday Strategy (W9–W12)
The trade-off: 3 weeks of build phase happen during the trip. Rather than fight it, we banked extra distance in W5–W7 (long run to 16 km pre-departure) and treat the holiday as a maintenance block.
2 runs per week minimum, more if you can. 1 mid-week 6–8 km + 1 weekend 10–12 km is the target. Hotel gym treadmills count. A run in a new city is one of the best ways to see it on foot.
Hold the long run at 10–12 km. Maintenance, not progression. Don't try to do a 16 km long run in unfamiliar heat — that's how injuries happen mid-trip.
Heat awareness. US summer in August will likely be hotter than Melbourne winter. Slow easy pace by 30–45 s/km. Hydrate aggressively. Run early morning if possible.
Post-holiday compression. W13–W15 is a compressed peak (+5 km long run per week). That's at the edge of safe — if the knee complains in W13, repeat the week instead of progressing.
Knee Pain Protocol
Red flags — stop running and see a physio: sharp localised pain, swelling, instability, pain going down stairs, pain that lingers more than 24 hours after a run, or pain that gets worse during a run.
Yellow flags — modify but continue: mild ache during the first km that fades, stiffness in the morning that resolves with movement, dull background awareness. Add walk breaks. Shorten the run. Run on softer surfaces.
Cross-training while the knee settles: cycling, swimming, elliptical, and rowing are zero-impact ways to keep aerobic fitness. 30–45 min at conversational effort 2–3× per week alongside the run plan.
Strength work that helps: single-leg glute bridges, side-lying clams, step-downs, wall sits, calf raises. 2× per week, 2–3 sets of 10–15 reps. Most runner-knee pain is a glute/hip strength deficit dressed up as a knee problem.
The W4 / W8 checkpoints matter. The plan has built-in cutback weeks where you reassess. Pain-free → build. Still sore → repeat the prior phase. Don't fight the calendar.
Race-Day Strategy
Don't go out fast. The first 5 km should feel embarrassingly easy at 7:30–7:45/km. Congestion near the start helps — let it slow you.
Fuel every 30–40 min. Practice gels on long runs from W6 onwards. Target ~60–90 g carbs/hr. Water at every station, electrolyte every second one.
The wall lives at km 30. If you've trained your long runs honestly and fueled on the day, you'll hit km 32 with the legs to grind home. The crowd inside the MCG carries you the last 400 m.
How the Adaptive Engine Works
Pace targets adapt. Easy and Long-run target paces are recomputed from a 28-day rolling average of your logged runs. As your easy pace drops, the targets follow.
Missed sessions trigger rules. If a session date passes with no logged run, it's flagged Missed. Two or more missed long runs in a row caps the next long run to no more than +2 km from your last completed long run.
HR drift is watched. If average easy HR climbs above 155 bpm, the Coach tab flags possible under-recovery and recommends a slower week.
Projected finish is live. Riegel's formula projects marathon time from your longest recent run. It under-predicts what you can do on race day with taper, but it's a fair fitness checkpoint.
